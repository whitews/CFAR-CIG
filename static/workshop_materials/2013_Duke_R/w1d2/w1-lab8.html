Multivariate linear regression

data(environmental, package = "lattice")
head(environmental)

##   ozone radiation temperature wind
## 1    41       190          67  7.4
## 2    36       118          72  8.0
## 3    12       149          74 12.6
## 4    18       313          62 11.5
## 5    23       299          65  8.6
## 6    19        99          59 13.8

plot(environmental)


lmozone = lm(ozone ~ radiation + temperature + wind, data = environmental)
summary(lmozone)

## 
## Call:
## lm(formula = ozone ~ radiation + temperature + wind, data = environmental)
## 
## Residuals:
##    Min     1Q Median     3Q    Max 
## -40.49 -14.21  -3.56  10.12  95.60 
## 
## Coefficients:
##             Estimate Std. Error t value Pr(>|t|)    
## (Intercept) -64.2321    23.0420   -2.79   0.0063 ** 
## radiation     0.0598     0.0232    2.58   0.0112 *  
## temperature   1.6512     0.2534    6.52  2.4e-09 ***
## wind         -3.3376     0.6538   -5.10  1.4e-06 ***
## ---
## Signif. codes:  0 '***' 0.001 '**' 0.01 '*' 0.05 '.' 0.1 ' ' 1
## 
## Residual standard error: 21.2 on 107 degrees of freedom
## Multiple R-squared:  0.606,  Adjusted R-squared:  0.595 
## F-statistic: 54.9 on 3 and 107 DF,  p-value: <2e-16

The fitted regression equation can be written as

ozone = -64.2321 + 0.0598 * radiation + 1.6512 * temperature + -3.3376 * wind

Prediction of ozone for new covariate values can be done with this equation or the 'predict' command.
