The fitted regression equation is given by

Log odds = 2.99 - 0.235 * Predictor

or equivalently

Odds = exp(log odds)
     = exp(2.99 - 0.235 * Predictor)

or

Probability of "Good" = 1 / [1 + exp(-log odds)]
                      = 1 / [1 + exp(-2.99 + 0.235 * Predictor)]

Since exp(-0.235) = 0.791, we will see 20.9% decrease in the odds of having “Good” for a one-unit increase in Predictor.

There are two ways of using the 'glm' command for tabulated data.

Response needs to be specified as a two-column matrix with the columns giving the numbers of successes and failures. 

myglm2 = glm(Outcome.tb ~ Predictor2.tb, family = binomial("logit"))

  • The log odds of low birth weight (low = 1) increases by -0.0167 (or decreases by 0.0167) for a one-unit increase in lwt, with all other variables being held at fixed values.

  • In other words, the odds of low birth weight increases by a factor of exp(-0.0167)=0.9834 (or decreases by 1.7%).

  • The odds of low birth weight is higher in the smoker group (smoke=1) than in the non-smoker group (smoke=0) by a factor of exp(1.0358)=2.8174 or by 181.7%.
